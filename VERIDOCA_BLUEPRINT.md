# 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

## Mission Statement
Veridoca.com is a free legal document template platform that empowers Americans to handle routine legal paperwork without attorney fees, while connecting users with licensed legal professionals when complexity demands it.

## Value Proposition
- 100% free downloadable legal templates in PDF and Word formats
- Plain-English explanations of every document's purpose and legal implications
- State-specific guidance for all 50 states plus DC
- Reviewed and updated by licensed U.S. attorneys

## AdSense Qualification Strategy
Veridoca avoids thin-content penalties through:
- Minimum 2,000-word substantive articles per template page
- Unique legal guidance content (not regurgitated boilerplate)
- Strong EEAT signals (attorney reviewers, editorial policy, citations)
- User-first UX with real utility before any ad placement
- Content differentiation: education-first, download-second structure

## Revenue Model
- Google AdSense (primary: $8–$22 RPM legal vertical)
- Attorney referral network (secondary)
- Premium template bundles (tertiary)
- LegalZoom / Rocket Lawyer affiliate (supplementary)

## 4.2 Sample Full Page Specification: Residential Lease Agreement

**URL:** /templates/real-estate/residential-lease-agreement/  
**SEO Title:** Free Residential Lease Agreement Template — Word & PDF (2026) | Veridoca

**H1:** Free Residential Lease Agreement Template (All 50 States)  
**Canonical:** https://veridoca.com/templates/real-estate/residential-lease-agreement/

### Content Outline:

**Introduction (250 words)**
Every landlord-tenant relationship deserves a clear written lease. This free residential lease agreement template covers all the essential provisions required by most U.S. states, including rent terms, security deposits, maintenance responsibilities, and lease termination procedures. Unlike generic forms, Veridoca's template is reviewed annually by licensed real estate attorneys and updated to reflect new state law changes.

**What Is a Residential Lease Agreement? (350 words)**
Define: legally binding contract between landlord and tenant. Cover: parties involved, property description, lease term, rent amount and due date, late fees, security deposit terms, utilities, maintenance responsibilities, pet policies, subletting rules, entry rights, and termination clauses. Explain the difference between fixed-term and month-to-month leases.

**When Do You Need One? (250 words)**
- Renting a house, condo, apartment, or room
- Converting a verbal agreement to written
- Lease renewals with new terms
- Adding or removing tenants mid-lease
- When security deposit amounts change

**Legal Considerations (450 words)**
- Federal Fair Housing Act requirements
- Security deposit limits by state
- Required disclosures (lead paint, mold, sex offender registry)
- Habitability standards
- Right of entry notice requirements (typically 24–48 hours)
- Anti-discrimination provisions
- COVID-era protections still active in some states

**State-Specific Requirements (500 words)**
Cover top 10 highest-population states with specific requirements:
- California: AB 1482 rent control, 60-day notice for long-term tenants, CCIAA disclosures
- Texas: No rent control, 24-hour entry notice, specific lock/deadbolt requirements
- Florida: 12% late fee cap, 15-day renewal notice, specific security deposit return rules
- New York: HSTPA protections, rent stabilization, 30/60/90-day notice requirements
- Illinois: Chicago RLTO for city rentals, specific habitability standards
- [Continue for remaining states]

**How to Complete This Agreement — Step-by-Step (550 words)**
1. Identify all parties (full legal names, not nicknames)
2. Accurately describe the property (address, unit number, parking spaces included)
3. Set the lease term with specific start and end dates
4. State the monthly rent, due date, and grace period
5. Define the late fee (check state cap)
6. Document security deposit amount and bank account information
7. List all included utilities and who pays each
8. Write out maintenance responsibilities for both parties
9. State pet policy, smoking policy, and guest policies
10. Include required state disclosures
11. Have all adult tenants sign and date
12. Provide tenant a copy within required timeframe

**Common Mistakes to Avoid (300 words)**
- Leaving blanks unfilled (creates ambiguity, courts may rule against drafter)
- Using one state's form in another state
- Omitting required disclosures (lead paint pre-1978 homes is FEDERAL)
- Not specifying what counts as "normal wear and tear"
- Vague pet policies that create disputes
- Failing to document pre-existing damage
- Not including a move-in/move-out checklist addendum

## 6.3 Fact-Checking Framework

**Three-Tier Verification:**

**Tier 1 — Source Verification**
- Every legal claim cites a primary source
- Acceptable sources: state statutes, federal law, court decisions, official government websites
- Unacceptable: Wikipedia, general news articles, other template sites
- All citations include: statute name, code section, last amended date

**Tier 2 — Attorney Review**
- All template pages reviewed by attorney with relevant specialty
- All state-specific pages reviewed by attorney admitted in that state
- Reviewer signs off: "I have reviewed this content and verify it accurately reflects [State] law as of [Date]"

**Tier 3 — Automated Monitoring**
- Google Alerts set for: "[State] [document type] law change 2026"
- LexisNexis or Westlaw alerts for statute amendments
- Quarterly content audit: all pages checked for outdated information
- User-reported error system: "Report an error" button on every page

## 6.4 Content Update Process

**Trigger Events for Updates:**
1. State legislature passes new law affecting covered documents
2. User reports an error via feedback form
3. Quarterly audit finds outdated information
4. Google Search Console shows page losing rankings (possible accuracy issue)

**Update Workflow:**
1. Change identified → flagged in content management system
2. Staff attorney assigned within 24 hours
3. Updated within 5 business days (48 hours for urgent law changes)
4. "Last Updated: [Date]" prominently displayed
5. Major changes noted in "Update Log" at bottom of page

```
Update History

January 2026: Added new state-specific requirements for Texas
November 2025: Revised NDA enforceability section based on FTC ruling
```

## 7.4 Ad Placement Strategy

**In-Content (after 2nd section):**
- 1x 336×280 or responsive ad unit

- At least 600 words of content before first in-content ad

**Sticky Sidebar:**
- 1x 300×250 or 300×600
- Sticky scroll: stays visible while reading

**Below Content:**

**Maximum Ads Per Page:**
- Template pages: 4 ad units maximum

**Prohibited Ad Placements:**
- No ads in FAQ accordions that mislead clicks
